Ongoing Independent Research

Human Factors in Safety Awareness Programs for Children and Youth

A Practice-Based Analytical Study
Author: Mohamed Amine Alhiane · Independent Research · February 2026
This work will undergo academic review by a supervising teacher upon completion.

Why I Chose This Research Question

During my experience working with children and young people in different educational and support settings, I observed that safety awareness is often treated as a transfer of information rather than a human-centered process. Many programs focus primarily on rules and instructions, while overlooking emotions, trust, and behavioral patterns that strongly influence understanding and action.

At the same time, I noticed that interactive and empathetic approaches consistently led to higher engagement and better comprehension. These observations led me to question why some safety awareness efforts succeed while others fail despite good intentions. As a result, I became interested in exploring how human factors influence the effectiveness of safety education for children and youth.

This research directly supports the goals of Youth Safe Tiznit by building a stronger evidence-based foundation for future awareness activities.
